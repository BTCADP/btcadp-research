Section 01

Indicator Definitions

TPM (Token Provenance Metadata)

The base data attached to each unit at issuance: mint date, mint-day BTC spot price, mint-day ₿C price, block age, and transfer count. TPM is the input layer. Every other indicator derives from it.

THI (Token Health Indicator)

The per-unit reserve health metric. THI is the percentage spread between the unit's mint-day BTC spot price and the current BTC spot price. Positive THI means the backing Bitcoin is worth more than the dollar obligation (black). Negative THI means it's worth less (red). A unit minted at $85,000 reads THI +11.8% at $95,000 spot and THI −17.6% at $70,000 spot.

For ₿OND units, THI reports two values: the saver condition (₿C progress toward target return) and the treasury condition (coverage ratio against payout obligation). Both must read positive for the bond to mature.

NHI (Network Health Indicator)

The aggregate health metric across the full unit population. NHI is derived from the distribution of THI values across all outstanding units. It captures the weighted average THI, the percentage of units in the red, and the depth of the deepest red positions. NHI tells you how healthy the network is as a whole, not how any individual unit is doing.

NVL (Network Velocity Limits)

Redemption velocity measured against defined thresholds. NVL tracks aggregate fiat redemption volume within rolling windows (24-hour, 7-day, 30-day) against historical norms. When velocity exceeds normal ranges, NVL tightens progressively. The longer elevated velocity persists, the tighter the limits become. When velocity returns to normal, limits relax along the same curve.

LCR (Lateral Conversion Rate)

The flow rate between product lines. LCR measures the volume of lateral conversions (₿USD to ₿OND, ₿OND to ₿USD, etc.) relative to fiat exit volume. A rising LCR during stress means holders are choosing to move between products rather than exit the system. A falling LCR means holders prefer fiat exit. LCR is both a health signal and a measure of how effectively the system's friction architecture is channeling pressure away from reserves.

PBP# (Profit Burn Priority)

The burn-order ranking for redemptions. When a holder redeems, the protocol burns the most profitable units first, protecting the reserve from draws on underwater positions. PBP# distribution across the outstanding supply tells you how deep the reserve cushion is before Ledger 2 is needed.

Coverage Ratio

Reserves (Ledger 1 + Ledger 2) against outstanding obligations. Calculated per product line. The ratio the public cares about.

Section 02

Network Defense State (NDS)

NDS is the system's overall readiness level, derived from the indicators above. Five levels. Counting up. Each level has defined entry conditions, defined exit conditions, and defined mechanism configurations.

NDS-1: Normal

All indicators within historical norms. No defense mechanisms active beyond baseline time-weighted fees. The dashboard is green. Routine operations.

Entry: default state. Exit: any primary indicator crosses its NDS-2 threshold.

NDS-2: Elevated

One or more indicators outside normal range but within manageable bounds. Early warning. Monitoring frequency increases. Time-weighted fees sharpen. No impact on ordinary users.

Entry: NHI drops below [threshold], OR 24-hour redemption velocity exceeds [X]x the 30-day average, OR coverage ratio drops below [threshold]. Exit: all triggering indicators return to NDS-1 range for [duration].

NDS-3: Stressed

Multiple indicators breached or a single indicator at an extreme value. Volume-triggered escalation fees activate. NVL begins tightening. BTC-default redemption becomes the primary redemption path. Lateral conversion fees reduced to incentivize product switching over fiat exit.

Entry: NHI drops below [threshold], OR sustained NDS-2 for [duration], OR 7-day redemption velocity exceeds [X]x normal, OR red unit percentage exceeds [threshold]. Exit: all triggering indicators return to NDS-2 range for [duration]. NDS never drops more than one level at a time.

NDS-4: Severe

Sustained stress across multiple indicators. Full defensive suite active. Escalating notice periods for large redemptions. NVL at restrictive levels. Lateral conversion actively incentivized (zero-fee conversion to ₿OND). Self-fortification: all fee revenue directed to Ledger 2 reserve deepening.

Entry: NHI drops below [threshold], OR sustained NDS-3 for [duration], OR coverage ratio approaches minimum acceptable level. Exit: all triggering indicators return to NDS-3 range for [duration].

NDS-5: Critical

Reserve thresholds approaching minimum. Maximum friction on all fiat exit paths. Lateral conversion at maximum incentive. Notice periods at maximum duration for large redemptions. All non-essential fee waivers suspended. The system is in full defensive posture.

Entry: coverage ratio drops below [critical threshold], OR NHI indicates majority of unit population in the red beyond [depth]. Exit: coverage ratio recovers above NDS-4 threshold for [duration].

Section 03

Transition Rules

Escalation is fast. De-escalation is slow.

The system moves up levels quickly because threats develop quickly. It moves down slowly because premature relaxation can trigger a second wave. Each downward transition requires the triggering indicators to remain in the lower level's range for a defined cooling period before the system steps down.

Single-step de-escalation.

The system never drops more than one level at a time. NDS-5 goes to NDS-4, not to NDS-1. Each step down has its own cooling period.

No human override.

NDS transitions are deterministic. IDMA monitors and reports. The protocol decides. No committee, no emergency vote, no discretionary intervention. The thresholds are published. The transitions are automatic. Anyone with access to the on-chain data can independently verify the current NDS level.

Section 04

Dashboard Concept

System-level panel

NDS level (large, central, color-coded), current NHI, aggregate coverage ratio, 24h/7d/30d redemption velocity with historical trendlines, LCR with directional arrow, total outstanding supply by product line.

Per-operator panels

Each certified operator gets its own panel showing: operator-specific NHI, coverage ratio (Ledger 1, Ledger 2, combined), PBP# distribution histogram (how the burn queue is stacked), outstanding supply, redemption volume, and operator-specific NDS contribution.

Per-unit drill-down (operator view only)

THI distribution across the operator's unit population. Red/black breakdown. Age distribution. Mint-price distribution.

Public vs. operator access

System-level data is public. Anyone can see the NDS level, aggregate NHI, and network velocity. Per-operator panels are visible to the operator and to IDMA. Per-unit drill-down is visible only to the operator and to IDMA during certification review.

Section 05

Open Questions

These are the decisions that need to be made by contributors, researchers, and eventually the Foundation Board:

Specific threshold values for each NDS transition. These require modeling against historical BTC price data and stress scenarios.

Rolling window durations for NVL (24h/7d/30d or different windows).

Cooling period durations for de-escalation at each level.

Whether NDS should be calculated per operator, per product line, or system-wide. Probably all three, with the system-wide NDS being the one the public sees.

How LCR incentives are structured at each NDS level (fee differentials between fiat exit and lateral conversion).

Whether the dashboard should be on-chain (fully verifiable) or operated by IDMA with published methodology (verifiable by audit).

The relationship between NDS and certification review triggers. At what NDS level does IDMA initiate a formal review of the operator?
