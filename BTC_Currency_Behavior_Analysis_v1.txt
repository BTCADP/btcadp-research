**Bitcoin Currency (BTCC)**

Empirical Behavior Analysis

Based on complete price history: January 3, 2009 -- March 14, 2026 \|

**Executive Summary**

Bitcoin Currency (BTCC) is the cumulative arithmetic mean of all Bitcoin
daily prices since genesis. This is an empirical analysis of BTCC
behavior across 5,719 days of price history (July 18, 2010 through March
14, 2026), establishing exactly how BTCC moves, why it moves, and what
conditions govern its direction.

BTCC is a predictably appreciating, incrementally deflationary unit of
account. Its daily movement is fully determined by a mathematical
identity --- not market sentiment, not monetary policy, not any external
oracle. It has posted positive appreciation in 5,712 of 5,719 trading
days, through every Bitcoin bear market on record, at annualized rates
ranging from +13% (2015) to +8,665% (2011).

**1. The Governing Formula**

BTCC is defined as the cumulative arithmetic mean of all BTCADP daily
prices from day 1 to day N. This definition produces an exact
mathematical identity that governs every daily price change:

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| **daily % change = ( spot_price / BTCC − 1 ) ÷ ( N + 1 )**            |
|                                                                       |
| where N is the current day count from genesis (day 1 = January 3,     |
| 2009)                                                                 |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

This is not an approximation. It follows directly from the definition of
BTCC:

**BTCC**ₙ₊₁ = (sum of all prices through day N+1) / (N+1)

**BTCC**ₙ₊₁ = (N × BTCCₙ + spotₙ₊₁) / (N+1)

ΔBTCC = (spotₙ₊₁ − BTCCₙ) / (N+1)

daily % = ΔBTCC / BTCCₙ = (spotₙ₊₁ / BTCCₙ − 1) / (N+1)

Two variables drive the outcome. The

**numerator** is the spot-to-BTCC premium. When Bitcoin\'s spot price is
high relative to the accumulated mean, BTCC appreciates more quickly on
that day. When spot approaches the BTCC level, appreciation slows. The
numerator fluctuates with Bitcoin\'s market price.

**The denominator** is the running day count. It increases by exactly
one each day and never decreases. This means the daily appreciation rate
is structurally decelerating over time regardless of Bitcoin\'s price
behavior. On day 1,000 the same spot premium produces roughly 6× the
daily appreciation it would produce on day 6,000.

**1.1 Directionality**

The formula has an important implication for direction:

-   **BTCC increases on any day when spot \> BTCC** (the premium is
    positive, the numerator is positive)

-   **BTCC is flat on any day when spot = BTCC exactly** (the premium is
    zero)

-   **BTCC decreases on any day when spot \< BTCC** (the premium is
    negative)

Bitcoin\'s spot price has traded below BTCC exactly seven times in
history --- all in a single week in November 2011. Given that BTCC is
the cumulative mean of all past prices, spot must sustain a level below
the entire historical average to push BTCC down. As the historical
average rises, this threshold becomes progressively harder to breach.

**Notable observation:** BTCC posted positive annual appreciation in
every single year, including during Bitcoin\'s three major bear market
years (2015, 2022, 2023). In 2022, when Bitcoin fell from roughly
\$46,000 to \$16,600 --- a 64% decline --- BTCC still gained +24%. In
2018, when Bitcoin fell from \$14,700 to \$3,700 --- a 75% decline ---
BTCC still gained +102%.

**This is not a coincidence.** Because BTCC is a cumulative mean, its
level at any point already incorporates years of prices far below
current spot. Even during severe bear markets, spot tends to remain well
above BTCC\'s accumulated historical average, keeping the numerator of
the formula positive.

**4. Daily Rate Behavior**

**4.1 Current range**

Over the last 365 days (through March 14, 2026), the daily BTCC change
has ranged as follows:

-   Minimum: +0.0386% per day

-   Maximum: +0.1114% per day

-   Mean: +0.0833% per day

-   Median: +0.0890% per day

Annualizing these figures, the current daily rates imply annual
appreciation of approximately 15--50%, with the midpoint around 35%,
depending on where Bitcoin\'s spot price is trading relative to BTCC at
any given time.

**4.2 Monthly variation**

Within any given month, the daily rate is relatively stable --- the
intramonth range in recent periods has been approximately 0.01--0.03
percentage points. The larger variation occurs across months as
Bitcoin\'s spot price moves. December 2024 and January 2025 (during
Bitcoin\'s post-halving rally toward \$100k) saw average daily rates
near +0.12%, while February and March 2026 (with spot around \$70k) have
seen rates near +0.04%.

This is consistent with the formula: a higher spot price increases the
numerator (spot/BTCC − 1), increasing the daily rate, while a lower spot
price compresses it.

**4.3 Negative days**

Seven negative days have been recorded across 5,719 days of history ---
all in the week of November 15--21, 2011. During this period Bitcoin\'s
spot price fell to approximately \$2.18--\$2.42 while BTCC stood at
\$2.46. This is the only instance in Bitcoin\'s price history where spot
sustained a level below the cumulative historical mean.

For BTCC to decline today, Bitcoin\'s spot price would need to fall
below \$18,716 --- a decline of approximately 73% from the current
\$70,400 level. Even in such a scenario, BTCC would resume appreciation
as soon as spot recovered above its current level.

**6. Observations**

-   BTCC has never had a losing year. Annual appreciation ranges from
    +13% to +31,674% across all years with data.

-   BTCC's daily movement is fully deterministic given Bitcoin's spot
    price. There is no randomness, no oracle dependency, and no
    discretionary mechanism. Any party with the BTCADP specification and
    trade data can reproduce BTCC to eight decimal places.

-   BTCC cannot be inflated. New days can only add to the denominator.
    The formula has no issuance lever, monetary policy setting, or
    parameter to adjust.

-   BTCC should not be described as stable or flat. The correct
    characterization is a predictably appreciating, incrementally
    deflationary unit of account with bounded daily volatility.

-   BTCC's appreciation rate is structurally decelerating. Each
    successive day brings greater predictability and tighter day-to-day
    ranges.

BTCC is a predictably appreciating, incrementally deflationary unit of
account with bounded daily volatility. It is the cumulative performance
of Bitcoin's price discovery process itself --- a function of every
trade that has ever occurred on the global Bitcoin market since the
first on-chain transaction.
